Works

In my practice, I look at affective environments by using a variety of tools, such as food, mobile-apps, paper models, video and walk. I am interested in interstitial spaces laying between everyday epistemological regimes and single perceptions.
The agency of naming. In terms of process, naming is an ephemeral intervention, that alters the counterpart being named.I often transpose my work to digital platforms with the aim of capitalizing portability and real time update. Situating projects on platforms as Google maps engages with questioning a priori truth-constructors of our time.

Happy Series - six happy maps

Six happy-experiments situate in four cities within three lands. In terms of process, the Happy-series involves people met on a chosen territory in a chosen time; I ask the encountered person to suggest me happy time-space coordinates, which i then map. Individual ephemeral affective genealogies of users visualizes in a specific moment in time within spacial coordinates (GIS). Scope of the project is to concretize individual ephemeral affective genealogies in form of temporary (im)possible ready-to-use travel guides; at stake is the blurring of boundaries between common knowledge of a site and imagination.Building on the premise that space might disclose affectivity, I have been mapping places named after happiness in the last three years. The notion of happiness is freed from exclusive reference to people, and it expands as quality to material environments; its experience does not reduce to an empirical truth.
