About



In my artistic practice and research over the last six years, I worked with drawing, food, installation, performance, photography, video and writing, exploring the agency that affectivity and imagination might or might not have on urban epistemic dynamics. Often, I work collaboratively and site specific, developing workshops, round tables and city excursions.
My interests span themes belonging to the history of science fiction, such as (mental) time travel, the human will to fly, the agency that naming concrete spaces after affectivity (often after happiness) might have on concrete environments. A central aim of my research is to look at the role of imagination in contemporary dominant narratives.
